Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about PDF to Word conversion.

Will the formatting be preserved when converting PDF to Word?

Text-based PDFs convert with good formatting preservation including headings, paragraphs, and basic layout. Complex multi-column layouts or heavily designed PDFs may require minor adjustments after conversion, which you can do directly in Word.

Can I convert a scanned PDF to Word?

Yes. Scanned PDFs are supported. Our converter uses OCR (Optical Character Recognition) technology to extract text from scanned documents and image-based PDFs.

What version of Word does the output file support?

The output is saved as .docx format, compatible with Microsoft Word 2007 and later, Google Docs, LibreOffice Writer, Apple Pages, and all modern word processors.

Is there a limit on how many pages I can convert?

No page limit. You can convert single-page PDFs as well as long multi-page documents. Larger files may take a few extra seconds to process.

Do I need to create an account to use the converter?

Absolutely not. No account, no email, no registration required. Just upload your PDF and download your Word file — that's it.
